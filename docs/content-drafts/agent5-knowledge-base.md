## 1. Referral Tracking

**How does my referral link work?**
Your referral link is unique to you, in the format
`gracelyn.edu/apply?ref={your-code}`. When a prospective student applies using
your link, the application is automatically attributed to you. Attribution is
tracked through the link itself and a 30-day cookie, so even if someone
applies a little while after clicking your link, the referral still counts.

**Why is my referral not showing yet?**
New referrals typically take 24 to 48 hours to appear in your dashboard after
the student applies. If it has been longer than that, contact the support
coordinator so they can check on it directly.

**How long does attribution take?**
Attribution happens at the moment of application, but it can take 24 to 48
hours to reflect in your portal dashboard.

**Can I refer myself, a family member, or someone in my household?**
No. Self-referrals and referrals of immediate family or household members are
not eligible for a referral fee and are considered a violation of program
rules. Referrals must be genuine, independent prospective students.

## 2. Referral Fees

**When do I earn my referral fee?**
You receive a referral fee after the student you referred applies using your
link, enrolls in a Gracelyn program, and remains actively enrolled for four
consecutive months. The referral fee is not paid at application or at
enrollment, only after that four-month mark is confirmed.

**How much is the referral fee?**
$100 for an undergraduate program, $200 for a graduate program.

**How is the fee paid?**
Through whichever payment method you selected during onboarding: PayPal, ACH
bank transfer (US ambassadors only), Wise (for ambassadors in Wise-supported
countries), or Tremendous virtual prepaid card. You can update your payment
method any time in Account Settings.

**What if the student withdraws or pauses before four months?**
No referral fee is paid if the student withdraws, is dismissed, or pauses
enrollment before completing four consecutive months of active enrollment.
There are no partial fees.

**Is there a limit to how many referral fees I can earn?**
No. There is no cap on the number of referral fees you can earn.

**Why do you call it a referral fee instead of a commission?**
Because that is what it is: an acknowledgment of your contribution to the
mission after someone you referred succeeds, not a sales commission.
Ambassadors are mission-driven participants, not sales agents.

## 3. Compliance and Portal

**How do I complete my compliance steps?**
You complete a combined compliance form with three parts: the Ambassador
Agreement, the Code of Ethics, and the Training Guide Acknowledgment. Each
part requires its own signature, all submitted together in one form.

**Why can't I see my referral link?**
Your referral link becomes visible in the portal only after your compliance
steps are fully complete. This is intentional: it confirms you understand the
program rules before you start sharing.

**Where is my portal?**
Your portal login is through the Gracelyn WordPress site. Once you are logged
in, your Dashboard Home is where you will find your referral link, your
ambassador recruiting link, and your activity summary.

## 4. Ambassador Recruiting

**How does my recruiting link work?**
Separate from your student referral link, you also have an ambassador
recruiting link, in the format `gracelyn.edu/ambassador?recruit={your-code}`.
When an educator joins the Ambassador Program using your recruiting link,
they are attributed to you as the ambassador who recruited them.

**When do I earn a recruiting referral fee?**
When an ambassador you recruited refers a student who completes four
consecutive months of active enrollment. The recruiting referral fee is $50
for an undergraduate-eligible referral and $100 for a graduate-eligible
referral. Both your recruiting fee and the recruited ambassador's own referral
fee are paid in the same cycle.

**How does the secondary fee work? Is there a limit to how deep it goes?**
The recruiting system has a maximum depth of two layers. You earn a fee for
students referred by ambassadors you directly recruited, but not for
referrals made by ambassadors that those ambassadors go on to recruit.

**What do I need to disclose when I recruit someone?**
Before a prospective ambassador applies through your recruiting link, you
must disclose that you participate in the program and may earn a recruiting
referral fee if they join and go on to refer students. Never guarantee income
or make specific earnings claims when recruiting.

## 5. International Payment

**How do I receive my payment outside the US?**
It depends on your country of residence, which determines what payment
methods are available to you:
- US ambassadors: PayPal or ACH bank transfer.
- Ambassadors in Wise-supported countries: PayPal or Wise (Wise transfers to
  your local bank account in local currency).
- All other international ambassadors: Tremendous virtual prepaid card,
  delivered by email and usable anywhere Visa or Mastercard is accepted.

**What payment methods are available internationally?**
Wise covers 80+ countries with direct bank transfer. Tremendous is available
everywhere as a universal fallback, no bank account required. PayPal is
offered where it operates, though its functionality varies by region.

**What determines which options I see?**
Your country of residence, which you provide during onboarding. You will only
see the payment methods that are actually available for your location.

**What if my PayPal payment fails?**
This can happen due to regional restrictions. The coordinator will offer Wise
or Tremendous as an alternative if that happens.

## 6. Program Rules

**Can I refer myself?**
No. Self-referral is prohibited and does not generate a referral fee.

**Can I refer a family member?**
No. Household and immediate family referrals are prohibited and are not
eligible for a referral fee.

**What are the program rules, generally?**
All referrals must be genuine, independent prospective students. Ambassadors
share information honestly, use only approved messaging when making specific
claims, and do not act as admissions counselors or make promises about
admissions decisions, employment, or outcomes. Violations can result in
disqualification, suspension, or removal from the program.

**Can I modify the marketing materials I share?**
No. Content ambassadors share with their networks comes from the approved
asset library and should not be modified in ways that change its meaning.

## 7. Portal Navigation

**Where is my referral link?**
On your Dashboard Home, once your compliance steps are complete.

**Where do I see my referred students?**
Your Referral Activity page shows your referral applications, enrollments,
pending referral fees, and paid referral fees. You will never see the names
or identities of the students you referred, only summary metrics.

**How do I update my payment info?**
Under Account Settings. Changes take effect on your next payment cycle;
anything already approved for payment uses the method on file at the time it
was approved.

**Where do I see my recruiting activity?**
If you have recruited other ambassadors, a separate section on your dashboard
shows your recruiting activity and any recruiting referral fees earned.
