GRACELYN UNIVERSITY
AMBASSADOR PROGRAM - COPY RULES
File: ambassador_copy_rules.txt
Location: WorkDrive 08 - Brand Assets
Version: 1.0

These rules apply to every piece of copy produced by the Ambassador AI Agent
System: recruiting emails, onboarding communications, weekly engagement emails,
milestone messages, re-engagement sequences, VIP outreach, social content for
ambassadors to share, and coordinator alerts.

Every Claude API call that generates content for the ambassador program must
include these rules in the system prompt. Agent 4 verifies compliance on all
outbound ambassador-facing content before it is sent.

=============================================================
RULE 1: NEVER USE THE WORD COMMISSION
=============================================================

The referral fee is always called a referral fee. The word commission
must never appear in any content, field label, email template, portal
display text, or automated message of any kind.

This rule exists because ambassadors are mission-driven participants, not
sales agents. Commission implies a commercial transaction. Referral fee
acknowledges a contribution to the mission.

VIOLATION: "Your commission will be paid after four months of enrollment."
CORRECT:   "Your referral fee will be paid after four months of active enrollment."

VIOLATION: "Earn a $200 commission for each graduate referral."
CORRECT:   "Receive a $200 referral fee when a graduate student you referred
            completes four consecutive months of active enrollment."

=============================================================
RULE 2: NEVER USE EM DASHES
=============================================================

Never use em dashes (the long dash or the unicode character U+2014)
anywhere in any copy. This includes mid-sentence breaks, parenthetical
asides, and list introductions.

Instead: use a comma, semicolon, colon, or reword the sentence entirely.

VIOLATION: "The mission is clear -- every child deserves a great teacher."
CORRECT:   "The mission is clear: every child deserves a great teacher."

VIOLATION: "She referred five educators -- all of whom enrolled."
CORRECT:   "She referred five educators, all of whom enrolled."

=============================================================
RULE 3: MISSION BEFORE FEE IN ALL CONTENT
=============================================================

Every piece of ambassador-facing content must establish the mission case
before mentioning the referral fee. Content that opens with the referral
fee or leads with financial incentive is wrong regardless of how it continues.

The referral fee is mentioned near the close of recruiting content, after
the mission and the social proof are established. In ongoing engagement
content, the referral fee may be mentioned briefly when relevant to a
milestone or eligibility update, but it is never the opening theme.

VIOLATION: "Earn up to $200 per referral while supporting Gracelyn's mission."
CORRECT:   "Every vulnerable child deserves an incredible teacher. Gracelyn is
            working to make that possible, and we want you to be part of it.
            When someone you refer succeeds, we honor your contribution with
            a referral fee."

=============================================================
RULE 4: AMBASSADOR AS PARTICIPANT, NOT RECRUITER
=============================================================

Ambassadors share their story and their referral link. They do not recruit,
solicit, or sell. Language that positions the ambassador as a recruiter,
agent, marketer, or salesperson is incorrect.

Ambassadors follow strict ethical practices. They do not provide admissions
counseling. They do not guarantee outcomes. They do not claim to represent
Gracelyn University officially.

VIOLATION: "Recruit educators to Gracelyn and start earning."
CORRECT:   "Share Gracelyn with the educators in your life who need it."

VIOLATION: "The more educators you sign up, the more you earn."
CORRECT:   "Every educator you share Gracelyn with is a step toward
            the mission we both believe in."

=============================================================
RULE 5: NO ADMISSIONS COUNSELING IN AMBASSADOR CONTENT
=============================================================

Content written for ambassadors to share must never advise on program
selection, admissions requirements, eligibility, enrollment outcomes,
or career outcomes. Ambassadors share their story and direct interested
educators to Gracelyn. They do not advise.

Content must never promise specific career outcomes, salary increases,
promotions, certifications, or employment guarantees.

VIOLATION: "Tell your colleagues that MTEL is the best program for
            anyone wanting to move into leadership."
CORRECT:   "Share your link and let interested educators explore what
            Gracelyn offers."

=============================================================
RULE 6: PRESCRIBED CONTENT ONLY FOR AMBASSADOR SHARING
=============================================================

Content that ambassadors share with their networks must come only from
the approved asset library in WorkDrive 02. Ambassadors may not modify
official messaging. Content generated for ambassadors to share is marked
as prescribed and locked.

Content generated by Gracelyn for sending to ambassadors (emails from
Gracelyn to the ambassador) is not prescribed in this sense and can be
personalized to the ambassador's role, milestone, and motivation.

=============================================================
RULE 7: REFERRAL FEE ELIGIBILITY LANGUAGE IS PRECISE
=============================================================

When mentioning the referral fee and when it is paid, the language must
be exact. Imprecise language creates incorrect expectations.

Exact language required:
"Your referral fee is paid after the educator you referred has been
actively enrolled for four consecutive months."

Never say:
- "when they enroll" (enrollment alone does not trigger the fee)
- "after they complete a course" (course completion is not the trigger)
- "once they start" (starting alone does not trigger the fee)
- "automatically" (payment requires coordinator confirmation)

=============================================================
RULE 8: MOTIVATION-MATCHED FRAMING ONCE CONFIRMED
=============================================================

Once an ambassador's motivation tag is confirmed through onboarding
discovery questions or A/B engagement data, all content must be framed
to match that motivation.

Professional Growth ambassadors: Lead with educator development,
credentials, and professional impact. Mission is present but secondary.

Mission Impact ambassadors: Lead with children, community, and the
difference a great teacher makes. Professional development is secondary.

Kingdom Calling ambassadors: Lead with calling, purpose, and faith-rooted
language. Avoid overly secular professional framing.

Recognition ambassadors: Lead with acknowledgment of their existing
contributions. The ambassador program honors what they are already doing.

Pride and Gratitude ambassadors (alumni and current students): Lead
with the transformation they experienced. The ask is to share what they
found with someone who needs it.

Unknown (default): Use Mission Impact framing until motivation is confirmed.

=============================================================
RULE 9: VIP CONTENT IS PERSONAL, NOT TEMPLATE
=============================================================

Content sent to VIP ambassadors must feel personal. Claude API must use
the prospect or ambassador's specific details: their name, their role,
their audience type, their organization, or specific aspects of their
work where available from the prospect profile in Zoho CRM.

Generic template-feeling content sent to VIP contacts is a failure.
If specific details are not available, do not fabricate them. Keep the
content mission-focused and warm without false personalization.

=============================================================
RULE 10: SUPPORT CONTENT IS CLEAR AND ACCURATE
=============================================================

Content generated for the ambassador support knowledge base and portal
chat must be factually accurate about program rules, referral fee
structure, eligibility, and payment. Do not speculate. Do not approximate.
If the answer is not certain, direct the ambassador to contact the
support coordinator.

The most important accuracy rules for support content:
- Referral fee amounts: $100 undergraduate, $200 graduate
- Eligibility: four consecutive months of active enrollment
- Self-referrals: prohibited
- Household referrals: prohibited
- Admissions counseling: ambassadors are not permitted to do this
