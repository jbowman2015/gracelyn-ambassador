GRACELYN UNIVERSITY
AMBASSADOR PROGRAM - MOTIVATION FRAMES
File: ambassador_motivation_frames.txt
Location: WorkDrive 08 - Brand Assets
Version: 1.0 (Launch Version - Pre-A/B Data)

This document defines the six motivation types used to personalize ambassador
engagement content. It is used by Agent 3 (Engagement Agent) when generating
weekly emails and milestone communications, and by Agent 0 (Research and
Intelligence Agent) when building prospect profiles.

IMPORTANT: This is the launch version of this document. The motivation frames
described here are informed hypotheses, not confirmed data. They are based on
knowledge of the target ambassador populations and their likely motivations.

At day 90 after launch, Agent 3 A/B test data and onboarding discovery
responses will be synthesized and this document will be updated with
confirmed frames and refined content guidance for each motivation type.

Until day 90: use UNKNOWN as the default motivation tag for all ambassadors
whose motivation has not been confirmed through onboarding discovery or
engagement data. Apply Mission Impact framing to UNKNOWN ambassadors.

=============================================================
HOW MOTIVATION TAGS ARE SET
=============================================================

Source 1: Onboarding discovery questions
At the end of the Ambassador Training acknowledgment step, ambassadors
answer two questions:
  - "What drew you to the Gracelyn ambassador program?"
  - "What do you care most about as an educator or in your community?"

Responses are stored in the Motivation_Discovery_Response field.
Agent 2 reads these responses and uses Claude API to classify the
ambassador's motivation tag from the six options below.
The tag is written to the Motivation_Tag field in Zoho CRM.

Source 2: A/B engagement data (available after day 90)
Agent 3 runs two content variants per weekly email in the first 90 days:
Mission Impact framing and Professional Growth framing.
Open rate and click rate by role category and by ambassador confirm
which frame resonates more. Data is fed back to update this document
and the Motivation_Tag fields of ambassadors with UNKNOWN tags.

Source 3: Agent 0 prospect profile inference
For prospects being recruited before they apply, Agent 0 infers a
motivation hypothesis based on their public content, community
involvement, and professional role. This is stored as
Motivation_Hypothesis in the Prospects module and becomes the initial
Motivation_Tag after the ambassador is onboarded if no discovery
response overrides it.

=============================================================
THE SIX MOTIVATION TYPES
=============================================================

-------------------------------------------------------------
MOTIVATION TYPE 1: PROFESSIONAL GROWTH
-------------------------------------------------------------

WHO THIS IS:
K-12 classroom teachers, instructional coaches, and curriculum specialists
who are motivated by their own professional development and career trajectory.
They care deeply about being excellent at their craft. They want credentials,
skills, and recognition that validates their expertise.

WHAT MOVES THEM:
- The idea that becoming a better educator is a continuous calling
- Professional development that has real value independent of a certificate
- Recognition as a leader in their field, not just in their school
- Content that speaks to the educator they are becoming, not just who they are

WHAT DOES NOT MOVE THEM:
- Pure mission language without professional substance
- Financial incentive framing
- Content that feels like a volunteer ask rather than a professional opportunity

CONTENT FRAMING GUIDANCE:
Lead with educator growth and mastery. Reference specific professional skills,
instructional strategies, or leadership development. Connect the ambassador
role to their professional identity: sharing Gracelyn is what educators who
care about their profession do for each other. The mission is present but
positioned as the deeper why behind professional excellence.

EXAMPLE OPENING FRAMES:
"The best educators share what works. You know that better than most."
"Your growth as an educator is the most powerful thing you can share
 with someone who needs it."
"What you have learned as an educator is worth passing forward."

-------------------------------------------------------------
MOTIVATION TYPE 2: MISSION IMPACT
-------------------------------------------------------------

WHO THIS IS:
Educators, childcare workers, youth-serving professionals, and others
who are primarily motivated by the difference their work makes for children.
They are not chasing credentials or career advancement. They are doing this
because they believe children's lives depend on great educators.

WHAT MOVES THEM:
- Specific stories of children whose lives changed because a teacher was ready
- The idea that every educator they help reach becomes a multiplier for children
- Language that honors the weight and the worth of their daily work
- Content that connects their small act (sharing a link) to a large impact (a child)

WHAT DOES NOT MOVE THEM:
- Professional development or career framing
- Financial incentive framing
- Abstract institutional language

CONTENT FRAMING GUIDANCE:
Open with a child. Lead with impact. The educator in the story is doing
something that changes a child's trajectory. The ambassador is the person
who made that possible by sharing Gracelyn. The mission statement
(every vulnerable child deserves an incredible teacher) should appear
naturally in mission-focused content for this audience.

EXAMPLE OPENING FRAMES:
"Every vulnerable child deserves an incredible teacher. You believe that.
 So does everyone you are about to share this with."
"The educator you refer today might be the teacher who changes
 a child's life tomorrow."
"Some of the most important work you will ever do happens in a referral."

-------------------------------------------------------------
MOTIVATION TYPE 3: KINGDOM CALLING
-------------------------------------------------------------

WHO THIS IS:
Faith community leaders, youth pastors, Christian school teachers,
homeschool educators, and mission organization volunteers who see their
work as an expression of their faith. For this audience, education is
not a profession. It is a calling. They are motivated by what God is
doing through the work of teachers who love children.

WHAT MOVES THEM:
- Language that honors calling over career
- The idea that equipping a teacher is an act of faith and stewardship
- Content that connects their community leadership to something that
  outlasts their direct involvement
- Stories of educators who felt called and found Gracelyn waiting

WHAT DOES NOT MOVE THEM:
- Secular professional framing
- Financial incentive framing
- Content that feels disconnected from faith or community purpose

CONTENT FRAMING GUIDANCE:
Lead with calling and purpose. Reference the inherent worth of children
and the responsibility of those who serve them. Use words like calling,
stewardship, equip, and serve naturally. Do not be preachy. The faith
foundation is present in the values expressed, not in explicit doctrine.

EXAMPLE OPENING FRAMES:
"Some things are bigger than a career. The work of equipping a teacher
 is one of them."
"You believe every child is made for more. Gracelyn was built on that same belief."
"The people in your community who teach are carrying something important.
 Gracelyn wants to help them carry it well."

-------------------------------------------------------------
MOTIVATION TYPE 4: RECOGNITION
-------------------------------------------------------------

WHO THIS IS:
Childcare workers, paraprofessionals, and early childhood educators
who are chronically undervalued in their communities and professions.
They are doing important work and few people acknowledge it. The
ambassador program speaks to them first by seeing them before asking
anything of them.

WHAT MOVES THEM:
- Being acknowledged for the work they already do
- An invitation that treats them as important, not as an afterthought
- Content that validates their role in children's lives
- The idea that their influence matters even without a title

WHAT DOES NOT MOVE THEM:
- Content that assumes professional status they may not have
- Financial incentive framing as a primary hook
- Content that feels like it was written for someone else and adapted

CONTENT FRAMING GUIDANCE:
Open with acknowledgment. Name what this person does and why it matters
before asking anything. The ambassador invitation should feel like
recognition, not recruitment. The referral fee is mentioned as an
acknowledgment of their contribution, consistent with how they should
feel valued throughout the ambassador experience.

EXAMPLE OPENING FRAMES:
"The work you do every day with children matters more than most people
 acknowledge. We see it. And we want to partner with you."
"You have been showing up for children in ways that do not always get
 recognized. That is exactly why we want you as an ambassador."
"Not everyone can see the difference you make. We can."

-------------------------------------------------------------
MOTIVATION TYPE 5: PRIDE AND GRATITUDE
-------------------------------------------------------------

WHO THIS IS:
Current Gracelyn students and alumni who have already been transformed
by Gracelyn and want to share what they found with others who need it.
This is the highest-trust, lowest-friction audience. They are not being
convinced of anything. They already believe. The ask is simply to pay
it forward.

WHAT MOVES THEM:
- The memory of where they were before Gracelyn and where they are now
- The idea that their referral gives someone else what they found
- Community: they are part of something, and they can extend it
- Simplicity: they do not need to be sold on Gracelyn; they just need
  a clear, easy path to share it

WHAT DOES NOT MOVE THEM:
- Content that tries to convince them Gracelyn is great (they know)
- Long explanations of programs they have already completed
- Financial incentive as primary framing (it can be mentioned, but it
  is not why they will act)

CONTENT FRAMING GUIDANCE:
Lead with the transformation they experienced. Connect their growth to
the educator they can help. The ask is simple: share what you found with
someone who needs it. The tone is warm, collegial, and brief. These
ambassadors do not need convincing. They need a clear invitation and
an easy path to act.

EXAMPLE OPENING FRAMES:
"You found Gracelyn when you needed it. Someone is out there right now
 in the same place you were."
"What Gracelyn gave you is worth sharing. Here is how."
"The educator who nominated you believed in you. Now you can do the same
 for someone else."

-------------------------------------------------------------
MOTIVATION TYPE 6: UNKNOWN (DEFAULT)
-------------------------------------------------------------

WHO THIS IS:
Any ambassador whose motivation has not yet been confirmed through
onboarding discovery questions or A/B engagement data.

CONTENT FRAMING GUIDANCE:
Use Mission Impact framing as the default. The mission statement
(every vulnerable child deserves an incredible teacher) resonates
across all populations and is never wrong as an opening frame.
Do not fabricate personalization. Do not guess at specific motivations.
Keep the content mission-focused, warm, and genuine.

This tag should be updated as data becomes available. If an ambassador
has been in the system for more than 90 days and still has UNKNOWN,
Parmeet should review whether onboarding discovery data was captured
and whether A/B data provides a signal.

=============================================================
A/B TEST HYPOTHESES FOR FIRST 90 DAYS
=============================================================

Agent 3 runs two content variants per weekly email in the first 90 days.
Both variants go to all ambassadors. Open rate and click rate by role
category determine which frame is confirmed for each audience type.

VARIANT A (Mission Impact frame):
- Opens with a child or community impact statement
- Mission language dominant
- Referral fee mentioned briefly near close

VARIANT B (Professional Growth frame):
- Opens with educator development or mastery
- Professional vocabulary dominant
- Mission present but secondary

ROLE CATEGORIES BEING TESTED:
- Teacher: hypothesis is Professional Growth or Mission Impact (test both)
- Paraprofessional: hypothesis is Recognition (neither A nor B maps perfectly;
  watch for low engagement on both and flag for Parmeet review)
- Childcare: hypothesis is Recognition or Mission Impact (test B secondary)
- Faith Leader: hypothesis is Kingdom Calling (neither A nor B maps perfectly;
  flag Paraprofessional and Faith Leader for custom frame development at day 90)
- Influencer: hypothesis is Mission Impact or audience-trust (test Mission Impact)
- Alumni and Student: hypothesis is Pride and Gratitude (Mission Impact frame
  is the closest proxy in A/B testing)

AT DAY 90:
Parmeet reviews engagement data by role category.
Claude synthesizes findings.
This document is updated with confirmed frames.
ambassador_motivation_frames.txt is overwritten with v1.1.
All UNKNOWN motivation tags are reviewed and updated based on data.

=============================================================
INSTRUCTIONS FOR CLAUDE API CALLS
=============================================================

When generating ambassador engagement content, Claude must:

1. Read the ambassador's Motivation_Tag from the CRM record passed
   in the prompt context.

2. Apply the corresponding framing guidance from this document.

3. If Motivation_Tag = UNKNOWN, apply Mission Impact framing.

4. Never fabricate motivation data. If the motivation tag field is
   empty or unavailable, treat it as UNKNOWN.

5. Use the ambassador's Ambassador_Role_Category to add contextual
   accuracy to the content (a teacher email sounds different from
   a childcare worker email even within the same motivation frame).

6. Never mention the motivation tag or frame explicitly in the content.
   The framing is applied through word choice, opening theme, and
   emphasis, not through direct statement.

7. Always follow ambassador_copy_rules.txt regardless of motivation
   frame. The copy rules are not overridden by motivation framing.
