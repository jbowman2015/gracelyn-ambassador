This document provides descriptions of all five Gracelyn degree programs
written specifically for an ambassador audience. Ambassadors share Gracelyn
with educators in their networks. They need to be able to describe each
program clearly, honestly, and in language that resonates with the educators
they are talking to.

These descriptions are distinct from the student-facing program descriptions
used in the AI Marketing System. They are written for the ambassador's use
in understanding what they are sharing, not for admissions conversion.

CRITICAL: Ambassadors do not advise on program selection. They share their
referral link and let interested educators explore Gracelyn directly. These
descriptions help ambassadors understand the programs well enough to have
honest conversations. They are not scripts for ambassadors to use when
advising educators on which program to choose.

=============================================================
HOW GRACELYN WORKS (share this context with ambassadors)
=============================================================

Gracelyn University is a DEAC-accredited online Christian university.
It serves K-12 educators through a nomination-based enrollment model.
Educators are nominated by their school leaders and then apply.
Gracelyn students complete their programs entirely online while continuing
to work in their schools and communities.

When an ambassador shares their referral link and an educator applies
using that link, Gracelyn tracks the referral. If the educator enrolls
and remains actively enrolled for four consecutive months, the ambassador
receives a referral fee: $100 for undergraduate programs, $200 for
graduate programs.

Ambassadors do not influence admissions decisions. They share the door.
Gracelyn decides who to invite through.

=============================================================
MTEL: MASTER OF TEACHING AND EDUCATIONAL LEADERSHIP
=============================================================

WHO THIS IS FOR:
Experienced educators who are being recognized for leadership potential
beyond the classroom. Teachers who are moving into instructional leadership
roles. Coaches, department heads, and campus leaders who want a graduate
credential to match their growing responsibilities.

WHAT IT DOES:
Equips educators for the transition from classroom teacher to educational
leader. Develops the skills to lead adults as well as students. Formalizes
expertise that many of these educators already have in practice.

HOW AN AMBASSADOR MIGHT DESCRIBE IT:
"It is for teachers who are already leading informally and want to make
that official. If you know someone who is the person other teachers turn
to, who principals already trust, this program was built for them."

WHAT AMBASSADORS SHOULD NOT SAY:
Do not promise specific roles, titles, promotions, or salary outcomes.
Do not say it qualifies someone for a specific position or certification.
Do not advise an educator that MTEL is the right program for their
specific situation. That is Gracelyn's decision to make with the educator.

=============================================================
MTED: MASTER OF TEACHING AND EDUCATIONAL DEVELOPMENT
=============================================================

WHO THIS IS FOR:
Teachers who want to deepen their instructional practice rather than
move into administration. Educators who are passionate about the craft
of teaching and want a graduate credential that makes them better in
the classroom.

WHAT IT DOES:
Develops master teachers. Takes educators who are already good and gives
them research-based frameworks, advanced instructional strategies, and
the professional vocabulary to articulate what they do and why.

HOW AN AMBASSADOR MIGHT DESCRIBE IT:
"It is for teachers who love teaching and want to be exceptional at it.
Not chasing a title. Chasing mastery. If you know someone who talks about
their students the way great teachers do, this might be what they are
looking for."

WHAT AMBASSADORS SHOULD NOT SAY:
Do not promise specific instructional outcomes or student performance
improvements. Do not say it is better than other graduate programs.
Do not advise on whether MTED or MTEL is right for a specific educator.

=============================================================
MAEP: MAESTRIA EN ADMINISTRACION EDUCATIVA Y PEDAGOGIA
=============================================================

WHO THIS IS FOR:
Spanish-speaking K-12 educators in the United States and internationally.
Teachers, coaches, and school leaders who serve Spanish-speaking student
populations and who want a graduate credential delivered in their language.

WHAT IT DOES:
Provides Spanish-speaking educators with the same quality of graduate
education available to English-speaking educators, delivered entirely
in Spanish. Designed for the unique context of bilingual and dual-language
educational settings.

HOW AN AMBASSADOR MIGHT DESCRIBE IT:
"It is a graduate program for educators who serve Spanish-speaking communities,
delivered entirely in Spanish. For educators who have been doing exceptional
work and want the credential that matches what they already know how to do."

WHAT AMBASSADORS SHOULD NOT SAY:
Do not promise bilingual certification or specific language credentials.
Do not advise on whether MAEP or an English-language program is right
for a specific educator. That is Gracelyn's decision to make.

=============================================================
BTED: BACHELOR OF TEACHING AND EDUCATIONAL DEVELOPMENT
=============================================================

WHO THIS IS FOR:
Educators who are working in schools without a four-year degree, or who
have a degree in another field and are building toward formal educator
credentials. Paraprofessionals, teacher aides, and classroom assistants
who want to become teachers.

WHAT IT DOES:
Provides a pathway to formal educator credentialing for people already
doing the work of education without the credential. Honors the experience
they have while giving them the academic foundation to do more.

HOW AN AMBASSADOR MIGHT DESCRIBE IT:
"It is for paraprofessionals and school support staff who are already in
the classroom every day and want to make teaching official. If you know
someone who has been doing this work for years and is ready for the next
step, this is the program that meets them where they are."

WHAT AMBASSADORS SHOULD NOT SAY:
Do not promise teaching certification outcomes. Do not promise that
completing BTED makes someone eligible for any specific certification
or license. Do not advise on state-specific licensing requirements.
Gracelyn handles these conversations with prospective students directly.

=============================================================
ATED: ASSOCIATE OF TEACHING AND EDUCATIONAL DEVELOPMENT
=============================================================

WHO THIS IS FOR:
Educators who are early in their journey and want to begin building
formal credentials. Paraprofessionals seeking an entry-level credential.
Individuals who are exploring education as a calling and want to take
a first step.

WHAT IT DOES:
Provides an accessible entry point into formal educator credentialing.
Serves educators who are not yet ready for a bachelor's program but who
want to begin their academic journey in education.

HOW AN AMBASSADOR MIGHT DESCRIBE IT:
"It is for someone who knows they are called to education and wants to
start somewhere. A first step toward a credential. If you know someone
who has been thinking about this but was not sure where to begin, this
is where you begin."

WHAT AMBASSADORS SHOULD NOT SAY:
Do not promise that ATED leads automatically to BTED or any other
credential. Do not promise specific career or compensation outcomes
from completing an associate degree.

=============================================================
REFERRAL FEE SUMMARY FOR AMBASSADORS
=============================================================

This summary is for ambassador understanding only. It is not prescribed
content for ambassadors to share verbatim with educators they are referring.

Undergraduate programs (BTED, ATED):
Referral fee: $100
Paid when: The referred educator enrolls AND remains actively enrolled
for four consecutive months.

Graduate programs (MTEL, MTED, MAEP):
Referral fee: $200
Paid when: The referred educator enrolls AND remains actively enrolled
for four consecutive months.

Payment is processed after Gracelyn confirms eligibility. Payment is
issued via PayPal or ACH based on the ambassador's preference set at
registration.

The referral fee is not paid at enrollment. It is not paid at one month
or two months of enrollment. It is paid after four consecutive months
of active enrollment. If a student withdraws before four months, no
referral fee is paid for that referral.

=============================================================
WHAT AMBASSADORS ARE NOT ALLOWED TO DO
=============================================================

This section is included so content agents know what language is out
of bounds when describing ambassador activity.

Ambassadors may NOT:
- Advise educators on which program to choose
- Advise educators on admissions requirements or likelihood of acceptance
- Promise specific career outcomes, salary increases, or promotions
- Claim to represent Gracelyn University officially
- Modify approved marketing materials
- Refer themselves, household members, or immediate family members
- Provide false or misleading information about Gracelyn programs
- Guarantee enrollment or outcomes of any kind

Ambassadors MAY:
- Share their personal experience as a Gracelyn student or as someone
  who knows Gracelyn's work
- Share their unique referral link
- Share approved social content and graphics from the asset library
- Answer general questions about what Gracelyn is and who it serves,
  within the limits described in this document
- Refer interested educators to Gracelyn directly for any questions
  about programs, admissions, or fit
