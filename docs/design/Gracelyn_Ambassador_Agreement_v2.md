**Gracelyn University Ambassador Program Agreement**

*Version 2.0 — May 2026 — Includes International Participation Terms*

This Ambassador Agreement ("Agreement") establishes the terms under which an individual ("Ambassador") participates in the Gracelyn University Ambassador Program.

By accepting this Agreement, the Ambassador agrees to promote Gracelyn University in accordance with the program rules and ethical standards outlined below.

**1. Program Purpose**

The Gracelyn Ambassador Program allows individuals to share information about Gracelyn University with prospective students and receive a referral fee when those individuals enroll and remain actively enrolled for a defined period. Ambassadors may also invite other educators to join the program and earn a secondary recruiting referral fee when ambassadors they recruit go on to refer students who meet eligibility requirements.

**2. Ambassador Responsibilities**

Ambassadors agree to:

- share information about Gracelyn University accurately and honestly using their unique student referral link

- use only approved messaging when making specific claims about programs, outcomes, or policies

- share their personal experiences truthfully and without exaggeration

- disclose their participation in the Ambassador Program when promoting Gracelyn

- comply with all applicable laws, platform terms of service, and advertising disclosure requirements in their jurisdiction

- recruit other educators to the program only through their ambassador recruiting link, and only using ethical and transparent practices

**3. Referral Link and Attribution**

Each Ambassador receives a unique student referral link in the format gracelyn.edu/apply?ref={code}. When a prospective student applies using this link, the application is attributed to the Ambassador. Attribution is tracked via URL parameter and a 30-day cookie. Ambassadors may share their referral link through any lawful channel.

**3A. Ambassador Recruiting Link**

Each Ambassador also receives a unique ambassador recruiting link in the format gracelyn.edu/ambassador?recruit={code}. When an educator applies to the Ambassador Program using this link, the new ambassador is attributed to the recruiting Ambassador. Ambassadors may share their recruiting link through any lawful channel.

The student referral link and the ambassador recruiting link are distinct. Ambassadors must not represent either link as the other, and must not use their student referral link to recruit other ambassadors or their recruiting link to refer prospective students.

**4. Student Referral Fee Structure**

Ambassadors are eligible to receive a referral fee when a prospective student they refer applies using their unique referral link, enrolls in a Gracelyn program, and remains actively enrolled for four consecutive months.

Student referral fee amounts:

- $100 for an undergraduate program enrollment

- $200 for a graduate program enrollment

Referral fees are paid only after the four-consecutive-month eligibility requirement is met. No referral fee is paid at the time of application, at the time of enrollment, or at any point before the eligibility period is completed. Gracelyn University is not obligated to pay a referral fee for a student who withdraws, is dismissed, or pauses enrollment before completing four consecutive months of active enrollment.

There is no cap on the number of referral fees an Ambassador may earn.

**4A. Ambassador Recruiting Referral Fee Structure**

Ambassadors who recruit other educators to the Ambassador Program through their recruiting link are eligible to receive a recruiting referral fee when an ambassador they recruited refers a student who completes the four-consecutive-month eligibility period.

Ambassador recruiting referral fee amounts:

- $50 when a recruited ambassador's referred student completes eligibility for an undergraduate program

- $100 when a recruited ambassador's referred student completes eligibility for a graduate program

The recruiting referral fee system has a maximum depth of two layers. Ambassadors earn secondary referral fees only for students referred by ambassadors they directly recruited. No fees are earned for referrals made by ambassadors recruited by ambassadors they recruited. There is no cap on the number of recruiting referral fees an Ambassador may earn.

Both the student referral fee and the recruiting referral fee are paid after the referred student reaches the four-consecutive-month eligibility threshold. Both fees are processed in the same payment cycle.

**5. Payment Terms**

Referral fee payments and recruiting referral fee payments are processed after the eligibility period is completed. Payment is issued through one of the following methods based on the Ambassador's country of residence and their selected payment preference:

- PayPal — available to all ambassadors worldwide where PayPal operates

- ACH bank transfer — available to ambassadors with United States bank accounts only

- Wise — available to ambassadors in countries supported by Wise international bank transfers

- Tremendous virtual prepaid card — available to all ambassadors as a universal fallback option

Ambassadors select their preferred payment method during onboarding and may update their payment information through the Ambassador Portal. Ambassadors are responsible for providing accurate and current payment information. Gracelyn University reserves the right to withhold or deny payments if program rules are violated.

| **Payment Method Availability** ACH bank transfer is available only to ambassadors with United States bank accounts. International ambassadors in Wise-supported countries are eligible for Wise transfers. All other international ambassadors receive payment via Tremendous virtual prepaid card. Payment method availability is determined by the ambassador's country of residence provided during onboarding. |

**6. Ethical Promotion Requirements**

Ambassadors must promote Gracelyn University honestly and accurately. Ambassadors agree not to:

- make misleading claims about programs, accreditation, or outcomes

- misrepresent tuition, program benefits, or enrollment requirements

- present themselves as official admissions representatives or Gracelyn employees

- make promises or guarantees about admissions decisions or employment outcomes

Ambassadors must use only approved messaging when discussing program details. Personal experiences may be shared, but they must not be presented as guaranteed outcomes for others.

**7. Use of Approved Marketing Materials**

Gracelyn provides an Ambassador Asset Library containing approved marketing materials. Ambassadors may use:

- approved social media posts and captions

- approved promotional graphics and images

- approved videos and video scripts

- approved written descriptions of programs and mission

Ambassadors must not modify official messaging in ways that change its meaning or accuracy. Ambassadors may share personal reflections alongside approved materials but must clearly distinguish personal opinion from official program information.

**8. Privacy Protection**

Ambassadors will not receive any personally identifiable information about applicants or students. The Ambassador Portal provides only aggregated referral metrics. Ambassadors agree not to:

- attempt to identify or contact students who applied using their referral link

- request personal application information from referred individuals

- disclose private information about students or applicants

**9. Referral Integrity**

The Ambassador Program prohibits manipulation of the referral system. Prohibited actions include:

- self-referral: submitting one's own student application using one's own referral link

- submitting fraudulent or fabricated student applications

- creating duplicate applicant records

- attempting to manipulate referral attribution or tracking

- referring immediate family members or household members as students

Gracelyn reserves the right to investigate and disqualify referrals that violate program rules. Confirmed violations may result in suspension or permanent removal from the Ambassador Program.

**9A. Ambassador Recruiting Integrity**

Ambassadors who recruit other educators to the program must do so ethically and transparently. Prohibited recruiting actions include:

- self-recruiting: applying to the Ambassador Program through one's own recruiting link

- recruiting immediate family members or household members

- recruiting inactive, suspended, or fictitious individuals

- misrepresenting earning potential or making income guarantees when recruiting

- recruiting at a depth greater than two layers from any originating ambassador

When recruiting other educators, Ambassadors must disclose that they participate in the program and may receive a recruiting referral fee if the person they recruit goes on to refer students who complete the eligibility period. This disclosure must be made before the prospective ambassador applies.

**10. Tax Obligations — United States Ambassadors**

Ambassadors participating from within the United States are responsible for complying with all applicable United States federal, state, and local tax obligations related to referral fee payments received through this program. Gracelyn University does not provide tax advice. Ambassadors should consult a qualified tax professional regarding their obligations. Gracelyn University will issue applicable tax documentation as required by United States law.

**10A. Tax and Regulatory Obligations — International Ambassadors**

Ambassadors participating from outside the United States are solely responsible for complying with all applicable tax, income reporting, financial, and regulatory requirements in their country of residence and any other jurisdiction in which they have tax obligations. Gracelyn University makes no representations about the tax treatment of referral fee payments under the laws of any jurisdiction other than the United States, and does not provide tax or legal advice to international ambassadors.

By accepting this Agreement, international ambassadors confirm that they have obtained or will obtain any necessary professional advice regarding their tax and regulatory obligations, and that they accept sole responsibility for compliance with all applicable laws in their jurisdiction.

| **International Participant Acknowledgment** If you are participating in the Ambassador Program from outside the United States, you acknowledge that: (1) Gracelyn University does not advise on tax or regulatory matters outside the United States; (2) you are solely responsible for determining and fulfilling any tax, reporting, or regulatory obligations that apply to payments you receive through this program in your country of residence or any other jurisdiction; and (3) Gracelyn University's obligation is limited to processing payment through your selected payment method — it does not extend to compliance guidance for your jurisdiction. |

**11. Program Modifications**

Gracelyn University reserves the right to modify the Ambassador Program at any time. This may include changes to:

- referral fee structure

- recruiting referral fee structure

- eligibility rules and requirements

- approved payment methods

- program policies and procedures

Ambassadors will be notified of material program changes. Continued participation following notification of a material change constitutes acceptance of the updated terms.

**12. Independent Participant Status**

Ambassadors participate in the program as independent participants. Participation in the Ambassador Program does not create an employment, partnership, contractor, or agency relationship between the Ambassador and Gracelyn University. Ambassadors are responsible for complying with all applicable tax obligations related to all referral fee payments received through this program, including obligations under the laws of any jurisdiction in which they reside or operate.

**13. Acceptance of Agreement**

By accepting this Agreement, the Ambassador confirms that they:

- understand the Ambassador Program rules and eligibility requirements

- agree to promote Gracelyn University ethically and responsibly

- agree to comply with the Code of Ethics and training materials provided

- understand the student referral fee structure and the ambassador recruiting referral fee structure

- agree to make required disclosures when recruiting other educators to the program

- understand and accept their tax and regulatory obligations in their jurisdiction, including for international ambassadors the acknowledgment in Section 10A

Acceptance of this Agreement activates the Ambassador account and enables both the student referral link and the ambassador recruiting link.

**Appendix A — Ambassador Code of Ethics**

*Gracelyn University Ambassador Program*

This Code of Ethics establishes the standards of conduct expected of participants in the Gracelyn University Ambassador Program. By participating, ambassadors agree to follow the ethical standards described below.

| **Combined Compliance Form Note** This Code of Ethics is presented as Part 2 of the combined Ambassador Compliance Form. A separate signature is required at the end of this section. This section is completed and signed in the same form submission as Part 1 (Ambassador Agreement) and Part 3 (Training Guide Acknowledgment). |
| --- |

**1. Commitment to Honesty and Accuracy**

Ambassadors must present information about Gracelyn University truthfully and accurately. Ambassadors agree to:

- communicate program information honestly

- describe programs using approved messaging when possible

- avoid exaggerating program benefits or outcomes

- correct any misunderstandings when they arise

Ambassadors must not make promises or guarantees regarding admissions decisions, graduation outcomes, employment results, or financial benefits. Only official university representatives may make such determinations.

**2. Responsible Representation of Gracelyn University**

Ambassadors are not employees or official representatives of Gracelyn University. When discussing Gracelyn, ambassadors must avoid representing themselves as admissions counselors, university staff, or authorized program advisors. Ambassadors may describe themselves as participants in the Gracelyn Ambassador Program and may share their personal experiences.

**3. Use of Approved Messaging**

To ensure accurate communication, Gracelyn provides approved marketing materials and messaging. Ambassadors must not modify official messaging in ways that create misleading impressions about programs or policies.

**4. Respect for Student Privacy**

Ambassadors must respect the privacy of applicants and students. Ambassadors will not receive access to student identity information and must not attempt to obtain it. Ambassadors agree not to request personal application information from referred individuals or disclose private information about students.

**5. Ethical Referral Practices**

Ambassadors must promote Gracelyn in an ethical and responsible manner. Ambassadors must not submit applications on behalf of another individual, create fraudulent or duplicate applications, attempt to manipulate referral tracking, or attempt self-referral. Referral fees are earned only when legitimate students enroll and remain actively enrolled for the required eligibility period.

**5A. Ethical Ambassador Recruiting Practices**

Ambassadors who invite other educators to join the program must do so ethically and transparently. Ambassadors must:

- disclose their participation in the program and their potential to earn a recruiting referral fee before the prospective ambassador applies

- represent earning potential accurately — never guarantee income or make specific income claims

- not pressure, manipulate, or mislead prospective ambassadors into applying

- not recruit family members or household members

- use only their official ambassador recruiting link when inviting educators to the program

Authenticity and honesty are the most effective approaches to ambassador recruiting.

**6. Financial Transparency**

Ambassadors must be transparent about the financial aspects of the program. When promoting the Ambassador Program or recruiting other ambassadors, ambassadors must not overstate or guarantee earnings. Referral fees are earned based on student enrollment and retention — they are not guaranteed compensation.

**7. Responsible Online Conduct**

Ambassadors must ensure their communications remain respectful and professional, reflect positively on Gracelyn University, avoid misleading statements about programs, policies, or earning potential, and avoid deceptive marketing practices.

**8. Reporting Concerns**

If an ambassador becomes aware of misleading promotion, referral manipulation, recruiting manipulation, or unethical conduct related to the Ambassador Program, they should report the concern to program administrators.

**9. Consequences of Ethical Violations**

Violations of this Code of Ethics may result in referral disqualification, recruiting referral fee disqualification, ambassador suspension, or removal from the Ambassador Program. Gracelyn University reserves the right to investigate suspected violations.

**10. Acceptance of the Code of Ethics**

By signing below, the ambassador confirms that they understand the ethical standards required by the program, agree to promote Gracelyn University responsibly, agree to recruit other ambassadors ethically and transparently, and agree to comply with program rules and policies.

**Appendix B — Ambassador Training Guide Acknowledgment**

*Gracelyn University Ambassador Program*

This Training Guide provides the information ambassadors need to participate in the Gracelyn Ambassador Program responsibly and effectively.

**1. Purpose of the Ambassador Program**

The Ambassador Program allows individuals to introduce prospective students to Gracelyn University. Ambassadors may receive a referral fee when a student they refer applies using their referral link, enrolls in a Gracelyn program, and remains actively enrolled for four consecutive months. Ambassadors may also invite other educators to join the Ambassador Program. A recruiting referral fee may be earned when an ambassador they recruited refers a student who completes the eligibility period.

**2. Your Two Links**

Each ambassador receives two distinct links:

- Student referral link: gracelyn.edu/apply?ref={your-code} — share this with prospective students

- Ambassador recruiting link: gracelyn.edu/ambassador?recruit={your-code} — share this with educators you invite to join the program

These links serve different purposes and must not be used interchangeably.

**3. How Referral Fees Are Earned**

A referral fee is earned when: a prospective student applies using your student referral link, the student enrolls in a Gracelyn program, and the student remains actively enrolled for four consecutive months. The referral fee is $100 for undergraduate programs and $200 for graduate programs. No fee is paid at the time of application or enrollment. Payment is processed after the four-month eligibility period is confirmed.

**4. How Recruiting Referral Fees Are Earned**

A recruiting referral fee is earned when: an educator you invited joins the Ambassador Program using your recruiting link, that ambassador refers a student using their own referral link, and that student completes the four-consecutive-month eligibility period. The recruiting referral fee is $50 for undergraduate program eligibility and $100 for graduate program eligibility. The recruiting fee system has a maximum depth of two layers.

**5. Payment Methods**

Payment is issued after eligibility is confirmed through one of the following methods:

- PayPal — available worldwide where PayPal operates

- ACH bank transfer — available to United States ambassadors only

- Wise — available to international ambassadors in Wise-supported countries

- Tremendous virtual prepaid card — available to all ambassadors as a universal option

Select your payment method during onboarding. You may update your payment information through the Ambassador Portal at any time.

**6. Using Approved Marketing Materials**

Gracelyn provides an Ambassador Asset Library containing approved materials including social media graphics, promotional videos, suggested messaging, and informational content about programs. Ambassadors are encouraged to use these materials to ensure that information about the university remains accurate and consistent.

**7. What Ambassadors Can Say**

Ambassadors may share their personal experiences with Gracelyn, why they chose the university, how the programs work, and how prospective students can learn more. Personal experiences can be powerful in helping others understand the opportunity.

**8. What Ambassadors Should Not Say**

Ambassadors must avoid making claims that could mislead prospective students. Ambassadors should not promise admission or acceptance, guarantee employment outcomes, misrepresent tuition or program details, or claim to be an admissions counselor or university employee. When prospective students have detailed questions, ambassadors should encourage them to speak directly with Gracelyn representatives.

**9. Respecting Student Privacy**

Ambassadors do not receive access to student identity information. The ambassador dashboard displays only summary metrics such as number of applications referred, enrollments generated, pending referral fees, and referral fees paid. Student names, email addresses, and application records are never shared with ambassadors.

**10. Ethical Promotion Expectations**

Ambassadors must promote Gracelyn honestly and responsibly. This means sharing accurate information, avoiding misleading statements, respecting prospective students' decisions, and using approved messaging when discussing program details.

**11. Monitoring Your Referrals**

The Ambassador Portal dashboard allows ambassadors to see their referral activity. Dashboard metrics include referral applications, enrolled referrals, pending referral fees, and paid referral fees. If you have recruited other ambassadors, a second section on your dashboard shows your recruiting activity and any recruiting referral fees earned.

**12. International Ambassador Tax Responsibilities**

If you are participating from outside the United States, you are solely responsible for understanding and complying with the tax and regulatory requirements in your country of residence. Gracelyn University does not provide tax advice for jurisdictions outside the United States. Please consult a qualified professional in your country if you have questions about how referral fee income is treated under your local tax laws.

**13. Ambassador Recruiting — Ethical Practices**

When inviting other educators to join the Ambassador Program:

- Always disclose that you are an ambassador and that you may earn a recruiting referral fee if they join and refer students who complete the eligibility period

- Never guarantee income or make specific claims about what they will earn

- Share your recruiting link only — not your student referral link — when inviting them to apply

- Respect their decision. If they are not interested, do not pressure or follow up excessively

**14. Tips for Successful Ambassadors**

Successful ambassadors typically share their referral link consistently, explain why they believe in Gracelyn's mission, share authentic personal experiences, and connect with individuals genuinely interested in education careers. Authenticity and honesty are the most effective ways to introduce others to Gracelyn.
